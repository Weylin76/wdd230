Last Visit information:

Elevation: Mount Crumpit towers at an impressive 3,000 feet above the level of Whoville, offering breathtaking views of the town and its surrounding snowflakes.

Slope Gradient: The north face of Mount Crumpit features a nearly vertical drop of 85 degrees, making it the ultimate challenge for the most daring sledders and Grinches with oversized, heart-filled sleds.

Annual Snowfall: This whimsical peak enjoys an average of 150 inches of snow each year, ensuring a year-round blanket of white, perfect for all your noise, noise, noise-making festivities.

Species Diversity: Home to over 500 unique species of Whoville flora and fauna, including the rare Who-Floribundus and the elusive, singing Who-Birds, contributing to the mountain's magical ecosystem.

Grinch Sightings: Despite being a reclusive character, there have been an average of 1-2 Grinch sightings per year, typically around Christmas time, as he contemplates the true meaning of Christmas from his cave.

Annual Whobilation Visitors: Each year, Mount Crumpit attracts up to 10,000 visitors for the Whobilation, a celebration of Christmas spirit, joy, and community, marking it as a premier destination in the world of Dr. Seuss.
